# Cinnamon Rolls

![Cinnamon roll](../images/cinnamon-rolls/cinnamon-roll.jpg)

Soft, gooey, cream-cheese-iced. The whole house smells like cinnamon for hours, which is the actual reason to make these.

## Ingredients

**Dough**

- 4 cups flour
- 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) instant yeast
- 1 cup warm milk
- 1/3 cup sugar
- 1/3 cup melted butter
- 2 eggs
- 1 tsp salt

**Filling**

- 1/2 cup softened butter
- 1 cup brown sugar
- 2 tbsp cinnamon

**Icing**

- 4 oz cream cheese, softened
- 1 cup powdered sugar
- 1/2 tsp vanilla
- Splash of milk

## Instructions

1. Warm the milk to wrist-temperature — warm, never hot, or it kills the yeast. Mix it with the yeast, sugar, melted butter, and eggs, then add the flour and salt. Knead until the dough is smooth and springs back when you poke it. Cover and let it rise until doubled, about an hour.

2. Roll the dough out into a big rectangle. Spread the softened butter edge to edge, then scatter the brown sugar and cinnamon over every inch. It should already smell like a bakery.

3. Roll it up tightly into a long log, seam-side down. Cut into 12 with a length of unflavored dental floss or a sharp knife, and nestle the rounds into a buttered pan, nearly touching.

4. Let them puff up for another 30 minutes until they're swollen and bumping into each other. Bake at 350°F for 22–25 minutes until golden and the centers look just set — pull them while they still seem a touch underdone.

5. Beat the cream cheese, powdered sugar, vanilla, and a splash of milk until smooth. Slather it over the rolls while they're still warm so it melts down into the swirls.

## Unsolicited Opinions

**Nate:** The trick nobody actually does: pull them when the centers still look slightly underbaked. They keep cooking in the hot pan.

**Ben:** Overbaked cinnamon rolls are just sweet bread. Tragic. Dry in the middle, sad all over.

**Nate:** And frost them warm, not hot. Hot, and the icing slides straight off into a puddle at the bottom. Warm, and it sinks halfway into the swirls where it belongs.

**Ben:** Carolyn cuts hers with dental floss. Looked completely unhinged, works perfectly — no squashed rolls.

**Nate:** Unflavored floss. She was very specific about this.

**Ben:** Minty cinnamon rolls. One time. We don't talk about it.
